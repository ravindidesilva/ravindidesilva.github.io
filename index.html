About Me

I am a 23-year-old final year undergraduate at the University of Kelaniya, reading for a degree in Teaching English as a Second Language. As part of the TESL Teaching Practicum/ Internship course we were required to select 6 or more students of our choice who can commit for a month-long English program and conduct the sessions via zoom. This portfolio contains the details of my experience as an intern including the lesson plans, testimonials, teaching philosophy and reflective summaries.

TEACHING PHILOSOPHY

“ The secret in education lies in respecting the student” – Ralph Waldo-

As I see learning is not just a single act but it is a process where we should step into many levels. For the success of this process the teacher has a great role to play. I believe that this role of a teacher should not be limited just to a speaker. A teacher should be and interactor, communicator as well as an active participant in the session. It is a skill of the teacher to make the lessons more interactive and effective. According to Ever Garrison a teacher is a compass that activates the magnets of curiosity, knowledge and wisdom in the pupils. As a trainee teacher I have experienced being a versatile teacher would help to make the lesson more effective to the students. This includes having conversations with students, playing fun games, give them chances to ask questions, peer discussions, group activities and many other class room activities.

My primary goal as a teacher, is to make my students stepping ahead each day than the previous day. I firmly believe that for this purpose the dedication and the skills of a teacher are the required key factors. This was experienced by me since I was a child where my school teachers put their fullest effort to shaped me into the person I am today. Being an undergraduate in this course (TESL) filled me with immense experiences and trained me to be a good teacher. What I have learnt from all my teachers and lecturers has taught me to be a versatile teacher who has a duty and responsibility towards his/her students.

One of my major responsibilities as a teacher is to keep my attention on every individual student. The students’ needs may differ from each other though they would sit in a one classroom. Thus understanding each student well is the responsibility of me in my teaching life. Our teaching practicum conducted through an online platform and thus a new form of teaching and learning came into effect. Teaching via online was found very interesting and time saving and it could be considered as a best option in a pandemic situation. The knowledge about the technical aspects of online teaching had to be gained prior to the lessons and it was a fresh experience to the teacher and the learner as well. One of my major responsibilities as a teacher is to keep my attention on every individual student. The students’ needs may differ from each other though they would sit in a one classroom. Thus understanding each student well is the responsibility of me in my teaching life. It is a great pleasure to see the improvement of the students’ and their enthusiasm in the classroom. And it is my duty to make them enjoy the lessons by creating my lessons in the way that they would actively participate. I firmly believe that being with students is a therapy to a teacher.

    REFLECTIVE SUMMARY

    Teaching through an online platform was a novel task for me because, this teaching practicum was my very first experience in online teaching. My very first task was to make sure that all my students would participated on time to the sessions. 30 minutes prior to the class, I generally sat with my lesson plan and revised how I should conduct the lesson. I also opened up the platform 5 minutes earlier, then the students could join the class on time. The students were very quiet at the beginning and the teacher talking time was very high on the first day. Other than answering some questions, students didn’t ask questions or didn’t make any comment on the lesson. To make them more attentive to the class I showed pictures and videos related to the lessons where ever required. When it was moving to the second day the students were quite familiar and comfortable with class where the student talking time was gradually increasing.

    Since this was an online practicum, the materials for the lessons should also be sent via online. While teaching the lesson I used to write important sentences, examples, new words and answers to the questions on the screen by using a word document. There I highlighted the impotent words with different colors. This function fulfilled the need of a whiteboard/ blackboard in the class room. The students also found it as a very effective method in teaching via online. Yet, when it comes to the activity sheets there was no way to distribute them to the class. The students were copying what was in the screen and then answered the questions. This was a little bit hard and time consuming task for both the teacher and the students. Also when I have to mark their answers I found it was difficult to mark them just by looking at the screen. To avoid these difficulties, I used to correct them while they were answering in the classroom and peer correction was found as a more effective and productive method of correction in online learning.

    In a virtual practicum the students and teacher don’t see each other physically. Thus sometimes the students became less interactive in teaching sessions. As a solution for this matter I used to add more fun games related to the relevant lessons where the students could think, talk with the teacher and the peers and understand the lesson at the same time. All the games had to be limited and should be formed in accordance with the virtual platform. The students couldn’t move here and there in the classroom yet they could talk more. One of the most productive games that I conducted in the classroom was the bingo game where the students have to fill the bingo chart within a given time period and present them to the class. And the students did enjoy and participated well in the game which was conducted in the lesson of “passive voice”. The students had to describe about a place which he/she selects using passive voice and the other students could ask questions using passive voice while guessing the place.

    The very first day where I tried the breakout room option it was not that much successful because the students complained that they were automatically leaving the room. After having a trial with the students the next day we could arrange it properly and the students got the opportunity to work in pairs and groups as in a physical classroom. I always encouraged

    students to come up with examples when I teach them. I explain the lesson first and ask examples from them. I found it very effective since I could correct their mistakes on that time. And from this teaching practicum it was evident that the students are more contributing to the activities where they can show their creativity. I asked them to draw a mind map after watching a video on pronouns and asked them to use color pens, pencils and different shapes to draw to the map. The final products of the mind maps were very creative and that was one of the most effective technique to teach a lesson.

    Teaching online was not an easy task. Even for the students it was difficult to sit in a one place by looking at a screen throughout the lesson. On the other hand, the major problem that we faced was the connection issue. Several days during the practicum there was a connection breakdown island wide which was beyond our control. Those days the connection was weak and the reports generated by the system also had some defects in time duration due to that issue.

    The students commented that this short period of time was very helpful for them in order to understand the subject and also they were very happy about the methodical order or the flow that the lessons had. Even the lesson materials were admired by them stating that they were all well prepared. Yet I myself as the teacher, felt that I should have make simpler activities in some lessons where it matches the students’ proficiency level. For an example in reading comprehension lesson the passages I selected were consisted with many difficult words which were too advance for grade 10 students. Overall this novel experience in teaching through a virtual platform lead us to reach the new level of education and this could be identified as the best solution which goes parallel with the new norms in a pandemic era.
